Lebak: The Coastal Gateway

From a hidden frontier to a bustling hub where the mountains meet the Celebes Sea.

A Thriving Frontier

Lebak was once known as the "Hidden Frontier," accessible only by long sea voyages. Today, it is a bustling coastal town famous for its vast mangrove forests and its vital link between the province's rugged interior and the Celebes Sea.

Background

Once a remote frontier accessible only by boat, Lebak is now a thriving hub. It is the site of the historic Katunggan Eco-Park, which serves as a sanctuary for diverse marine life and a symbol of the town's commitment to environmental preservation.

The Vibe

It feels like the "Old Philippines"—salt air, vast mangrove canopies, and a slower pace of life governed by the tides. It is a place where tranquility and nature meet, perfect for those looking to reconnect with the coastal wild.
