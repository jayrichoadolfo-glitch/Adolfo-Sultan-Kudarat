The Sultan’s Legacy

Discover the warrior-king legacy and the birth of a province carved from history.

Sultan Kudarat was officially born on November 22, 1973, via Presidential Decree No. 341. It was carved from the "Empire Province of Cotabato" to create a distinct identity for the region and its people.

The province is named after Sultan Muhammad Dipatuan Kudarat, a 17th-century hero who united Mindanao and successfully defended the archipelago against Spanish colonization for over 50 years.

It is a province named after a warrior-king, representing a legacy of sovereignty and unyielding courage. It stands as a permanent monument to the triumph of local resistance against foreign rule, serving as a testament to the bravery of the Sultanate.
