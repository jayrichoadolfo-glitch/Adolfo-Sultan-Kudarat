Proper Etiquette

Sultan Kudarat is a land of deep faith and tradition. Respecting the local customs of the "Tri-people" (Muslims, Christians, and Lumads) ensures a warm welcome and a meaningful experience for every traveler.

Social Respect

Dress Code: When visiting Mosques or indigenous villages, dress modestly. Ensure shoulders and knees are covered as a sign of respect for local traditions.

Photography: Always ask for permission before taking photos of locals, especially during prayer times, tribal rituals, or within sacred spaces.

Dining & Greetings

The "Salam" Greeting: A simple "Assalamu Alaikum" (Peace be unto you) is the traditional greeting in Muslim communities and goes a long way in building rapport.

Alcohol & Food: Be mindful that many areas are strictly Halal-observant. Avoid bringing pork or alcohol into Muslim neighborhoods or during shared community meals.

Quick Info

Religion

Islam, Christianity, and Indigenous Beliefs

Key Tip

Respect prayer times (5 times a day). During these periods, noise levels should be kept to a minimum near Mosques.

Vibe

Conservative yet exceptionally hospitable. A little respect earns a lot of friendship.
