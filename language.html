Dialects

While Hiligaynon (Ilonggo) is the primary tongue used in commerce and daily life, the province is a linguistic treasure. In the highlands, you will hear the ancient chants of the Manobo; in the coastal towns, the melodic Maguindanaon; and in the north, the sharp, rhythmic Ilocano.

In Sultan Kudarat, you don't just speak a language; you speak a history of migration and co-existence.

Most locals are trilingual, easily shifting between their native dialect, Tagalog, and English to welcome visitors.
