Lebak: The Coastal Gateway

Lebak was once the "Hidden Frontier," accessible only by a long sea voyage. Today, it is a bustling coastal town known for its vast mangrove forests and the Katunggan Eco-Park, which features a stunning bamboo boardwalk stretching out to the sea.

Background

Once a remote frontier accessible only by boat, Lebak is now a thriving coastal hub. It is the site of the historic Katunggan Eco-Park, serving as a vital link between the mountains and the Celebes Sea.

The Vibe

It feels like the "Old Philippines"—vast mangrove forests, salt air, and a slower pace of life governed by the tides. It is a place where tranquility and nature meet.
