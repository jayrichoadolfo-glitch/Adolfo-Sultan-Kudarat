Proper Etiquette

Sultan Kudarat is a land of deep faith and tradition. Respecting the local customs of the "Tri-people" (Muslims, Christians, and Lumads) ensures a warm welcome and a meaningful experience.

Dress Code: When visiting Mosques or indigenous villages, dress modestly. Cover shoulders and knees.

Photography: Always ask for permission before taking photos of locals, especially during prayer times or tribal rituals.

The "Salam" Greeting: A simple "Assalamu Alaikum" (Peace be unto you) goes a long way in Muslim communities.

Alcohol & Food: Be mindful that many areas are Halal-observant. Avoid bringing pork or alcohol into strictly Muslim neighborhoods.

Quick Info

Religion: Islam & Christianity.

Key Tip: Respect prayer times (5 times a day).
