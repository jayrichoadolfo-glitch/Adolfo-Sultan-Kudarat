Map of Sultan Kudarat

The province is a geographical masterpiece of over 5,000 square kilometers. To the West, the coastline touches the Celebes Sea, guarded by the rugged Daguma Mountain Range. To the East, the land opens into the fertile Allah Valley, a vast agricultural basin.

A land of dramatic contrasts. To the West, the Daguma Mountain Range drops sharply into the Celebes Sea. To the East, the fertile Allah Valley provides a flat, golden basin for agriculture.

This "Wall of Mountains" creates a natural barrier that protects the inland plains, resulting in a climate that is perfect for both coastal fishing and inland farming.
