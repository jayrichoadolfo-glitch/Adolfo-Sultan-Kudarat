Proper Etiquette

Sultan Kudarat is a land of deep faith and tradition. Respecting local customs ensures a meaningful experience.

Respecting the local customs of the "Tri-people" (Muslims, Christians, and Lumads) ensures a warm welcome and a meaningful experience for every traveler.

Social Respect

Dress Code: When visiting Mosques or indigenous villages, dress modestly. Ensure shoulders and knees are covered as a sign of respect.

Photography: Always ask for permission before taking photos of locals, especially during prayer times or tribal rituals.

Dining & Greetings

The "Salam" Greeting: A simple "Assalamu Alaikum" (Peace be unto you) is traditional in Muslim communities and builds rapport instantly.

Alcohol & Food: Be mindful that many areas are strictly Halal-observant. Avoid bringing pork or alcohol into Muslim neighborhoods.

Core Values

The culture here is built on the foundation of Kalinga (Care) and Respeto (Respect). While the communities are exceptionally hospitable, they appreciate travelers who take the time to understand their boundaries and sacred traditions.

Key Tip

Respect prayer times (5 times a day). Keep noise levels to a minimum near Mosques during these periods.

The Vibe

Conservative yet exceptionally hospitable. A little respect earns a lifetime of friendship.
